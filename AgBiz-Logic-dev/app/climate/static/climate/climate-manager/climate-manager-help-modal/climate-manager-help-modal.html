Climate Manager Help

With AgBizClimate, you can do two things. First, with the Seasonal Climate Forecast, you can explore whether to expect warmer or cooler or wetter or drier conditions during the next several months. With that information, you can estimate how your yields and operations may respond to such weather conditions in the coming months and view the effects on your net returns. Secondly, with the Future Climate Projection, you can step into the climate of 20–30 years from present to consider how your current enterprises and operations will continue to serve you under future average conditions that may be different from what you’ve experienced in the past.

Seasonal Climate Forecast can support you with decisions you’ll make for the current year’s crop to maximize net returns. Future Climate Projection can support you with long-range planning decisions you may want to begin considering now in order to maintain profitable operations 20-30 years from now.

The next steps for both Seasonal Climate Forecast and Future Climate Projection is to use AgBizProfit to explore adaptations that could mitigate potential net losses during the current season, or that could sustain profitable operations decades into the future.

Climate prediction: A climate prediction or climate forecast is the result of an attempt to produce (starting from a particular state of the climate system) an estimate of the actual evolution of the climate in the future, for example, at seasonal, interannual, or decadal time scales. Because the future evolution of the climate system may be highly sensitive to initial conditions, such predictions are usually probabilistic in nature. (IPCC Glossary, 2014, link.)
Climate projection: A climate projection is the simulated response of the climate system to a scenario of future emission or concentration of greenhouse gases and aerosols, generally derived using climate models. Climate projections are distinguished from climate predictions by their dependence on the emission/concentration/radiative forcing scenario used, which is in turn based on assumptions concerning, for example, future socioeconomic and technological developments that may or may not be realized. (IPCC, 2013 Glossary, link.)
